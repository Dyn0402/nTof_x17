# Engineer package — figure guide

Curated figures from the June 2026 Saclay cosmic-bench characterization of the MX17
micro-TPC Micromegas detectors, selected for a ~20 min construction-and-performance
talk. Detector letters: **A = det3, B = det2, C = det6, D = det7, E = det4**
(letters appear in filenames as e.g. `det3A`). Every PNG has an identically named
PDF twin (lossless embed) for LaTeX/Beamer. Full write-ups live in
`../source_reports/`.

Numbers quoted from `mx_june_cosmic_qa/JUNE_RESULTS_SUMMARY.md` (2026-07-14, M3
reference recipe χ²<1.0 & NClus=4): efficiency within 5 mm — det3 92.9 %, det2 91.3 %,
det6 57.8 %, det7 43.1 %, det4 20.7 %; core spatial σ 0.44–0.67 mm; angular σ ≈ 1.7–3.9°
(det6 plateau still broken, hardware defect). Superseded (χ²<5, do not quote): det3
88.8 %, det2 87.0 %, det6 55.7 %, det7 36.4 %, det4 20.0 %; core σ 0.6–0.9 mm.

**2026-07-14 regeneration status:** figures 10–34 and 44 (spatial residuals, hybrid
tracking, charge sharing, efficiency maps/breakdowns, HV scans, spark dead-time) are
regenerated on the new recipe. Figures 40–43 (spark characterization, det3_spark_analysis)
are unaffected by design (loose M3 cut, not the golden recipe) and were not touched.
Figures 50–52 (time resolution) were checked and are byte-identical to a fresh 42-rerun
-- no update needed (time resolution doesn't depend on M3 position precision). Figures
60–61 (charge balance) were regenerated via `38b_charge_balance_report_figs.py`. Figures
70–73 (drift velocity / gas survey) and 80 (edge/fringe field) were **not** re-run this
pass (scripts 21/23/30/32 weren't part of the 2026-07-14 waveform-chain rerun) -- treat
as pre-recipe-change and not yet re-verified, though not expected to shift materially
(these don't depend on M3 position precision the way efficiency/residuals do).

Figures marked **[multi-panel / QA-styled]** are analysis dashboards — informative
but busy; consider cropping a single panel for slides.

### 02-microtpc-principle-schematic.png
Two-panel schematic of the micro-TPC principle: left, a muon crossing the 30 mm
drift gap with ionization electrons drifting down to the mesh/resistive strips
(shaded band showing charge lost to attachment near the cathode); right, the
per-strip time-vs-position ladder whose slope gives the track angle via the drift
velocity.
Caption: *Micro-TPC mode: each strip times its drift electrons, so one 30 mm gap gives a 3D track segment.*
Source: `mx_june_cosmic_qa/report_det3_weekend/figures/diagram_principle.png`

### 03-charge-sharing-schematic.png
Three-panel schematic of resistive-strip charge sharing: (a) avalanche charge
spreading ~50 % onto neighbouring strips, (b) example waveforms showing why a
deep-end strip fires early when it inherits its neighbour's shared charge, (c) the
resulting S-shaped distortion of the time–position ladder that biases a naive
straight-line velocity fit low.
Caption: *Resistive strips share ~half the avalanche charge with neighbours — measured, and corrected offline ("unsharing").*
Source: `mx_june_cosmic_qa/report_det3_weekend/figures/diagram_sharing.png`

### 04-angle-bias-schematic.png
Side-by-side schematic of a vertical vs a 25° inclined track in the drift gap,
showing that the recorded time span is fixed by the visible drift column while the
cluster width adds a constant spreading term — the geometric reason the raw
micro-TPC angle overestimates small angles.
Caption: *Why raw micro-TPC angles are biased at small track angle — geometry of cluster width vs time span.*
Source: `mx_june_cosmic_qa/report_det3_weekend/figures/diagram_offset.png`

### 05-charge-routing-schematic.png
Clean vertical-section cartoon of the chamber stack: drift gap, mesh, avalanche
(×10⁴), pixelated resistive top layer, and the X and Y strip layers underneath,
with the avalanche charge routed to both layers and the balance fraction
f = qX/(qX+qY) defined in a box.
Caption: *The pixelated resistive top layer routes the avalanche charge to both orthogonal strip layers.*
Source: `mx_june_cosmic_qa/report_charge_balance/figs/schematic_routing.png`

### 06-timing-principle-schematic.png
Minimal schematic of the timing measurement: an inclined muon track in the gap,
the shortest-drift point defining the leading edge, and the stacked X/Y strip
layers that independently timestamp the same drifting electrons.
Caption: *One drift gap, two orthogonal readouts — both timestamp the same electrons, giving a built-in time-resolution measurement.*
Source: `mx_june_cosmic_qa/report_time_resolution/figs/geometry_schematic.png`

### 07-det3A-event-3d-display.png
A single real cosmic-muon event (detector A, event 25066, ~20°) rendered in 3-D:
each strip signal placed at its strip position (X/Y) and at the depth implied by
its electron drift time, colored by amplitude (circle = X plane, square = Y
plane). The green line is the **independent M3 telescope track** extrapolated
into the chamber — it threads the charge cloud (match 0.3 mm), a direct 3-D
check of reconstruction + alignment. Faint planes mark the mesh (z=0) and the
30 mm drift window. Display events are picked near the tight end of the match
distribution (the full-sample core is 0.6–0.8 mm/axis, centered at zero — no
misalignment); `--max-resid` controls the cut.
Caption: *One muon in 3-D: the drift-time charge cloud, with the independent reference telescope track running through it.*
Source: generated by `../make_event_displays_3d.py --figures` (hero, `--hero-band`)

### 08-det3A-event-3d-gallery.png
2×2 gallery of four 3-D events spanning ~6–20° (events 12848/34028/24718/25066),
same construction as fig 07, each a tight (0.1–0.3 mm) reference match. Shows the
reference track threading the charge cloud from near-vertical to inclined. Any

Caption: *Cosmic muons in 3-D across angles — the M3 reference track threads the charge cloud in every case.*
Source: generated by `../make_event_displays_3d.py --figures` (gallery, `--gallery-bands`)

### 09-det3A-unsharing-depth-bias.png
**[systematics / reconstruction]** Population proof that the hit-level
charge-sharing "unsharing" removes the depth-dependent angle bias. Signed
distance of core strips from the M3 reference line vs drift depth, RAW
production ladder vs UNSHARED (state-of-the-art), ~500 muons. The raw bias
grows with depth (the ~4° too-steep ladder = charge sharing); unsharing
flattens the depth trend (residual slope ≈ 0), the remaining constant offset is
a re-alignment effect (the alignment was fit on raw hits) and the residual
diffusion floor is removed by the per-plane angle calibration (script 28).
Rendered by the same tool that runs the deconvolution (`sota_reco.py`).
Caption: *Hit-level unsharing removes the charge-sharing depth bias — the state-of-the-art reconstruction the angle numbers rely on.*
Source: generated by `../make_event_displays_3d.py --compare` (needs decoded_root)

### 16-det3A-hybrid-angular-resolution-vs-angle.png

honest ablation of what the head-on algorithm buys — NOT the raw production
time-fit (which sat ~15° everywhere and never converged; deliberately not shown).
Caption: *The hybrid tracker delivers a uniform ≈1.7° angular resolution at every track angle — the vertical-track blind spot is gone.*
